Cinnamon Rolls

Description

Originating from XVI century Germany, cinnamon rolls are a popular treat in both Northern Europe and North America. The process of preparing yeasted dough may seem daunting at first, but every recipe will be easier when split into basic steps.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Rapid-rise yeast (1 package)

Filling

  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon

Icing

  • 3 ounces softened cream cheese
  • 1 tbsp softened butter
  • 1 tbsp milk
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups confectioners' sugar

Steps

The Rolls

  1. Heat milk, water, and 1/4 cup butter over medium heat for 5 minutes (or until bubbling).
  2. Let it cool to 120—130°F (49—54°C), which should take about 1—2 minutes. It is recommended to use a thermometer.
  3. Mix the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast in a separate bowl.
  4. Mix the egg into the milk.
  5. Pour the milk and 1/3 of the dry mixture in a mixing bowl. Beat until moistened.
  6. Slowly add in the rest of the dry mixture until the dough has formed.
  7. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 8 minutes. Cover and let it rise in a dry, warm place for 1—2 hours or until doubled in size.
  8. Punch the dough down and roll it on a lightly floured surface into a 16x12-inch rectangle.
  9. Spread 1/4 cup softened butter over dough.
  10. Mix brown sugar and cinnamon together. Sprinkle over the dough.
  11. Roll the dough up from the short side. Cut into even rolls.
  12. Place onto a greased baking dish and let them rest from 8 hours to overnight.
  13. Bake in a preheated oven on 375°F (190°C) for about 17 minutes.

The Icing

  1. Beat the cream cheese, butter, milk, and vanilla extract together until creamy.
  2. Slowly add confectioners' sugar and mix until smooth.
  3. Let the rolls cool slightly and spread the icing over them.
